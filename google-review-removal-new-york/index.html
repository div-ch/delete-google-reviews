Legal Context

New York Defamation Law and Online Reviews

New York has strong anti-defamation laws that protect businesses from false statements of fact published online. Under New York Civil Rights Law and common law, a business can pursue legal action against individuals who publish false statements that damage the business's reputation. New York courts have applied these principles to online reviews, establishing that Google reviews containing provably false factual claims can constitute actionable defamation.

New York's Approach to Online Speech

New York courts balance free speech protections with the right of businesses to be free from false and damaging statements. Reviews that express genuine opinions are protected. Reviews that state specific false facts, such as falsely claiming a business committed fraud, violated health codes, or engaged in illegal activity, are not protected speech when the claims are demonstrably false. This legal framework provides strong support for review removal efforts in New York, and our documented case files benefit from the detailed analysis that New York's legal standards require.

Policy-Based Removal Is Faster Than Litigation

While New York law provides robust legal remedies for defamatory reviews, pursuing removal through the court system in New York can take months and cost thousands in legal fees. Our approach focuses on Google's content policies, achieving removal in 3 to 21 business days at a fraction of the cost of litigation. For NYC businesses that need damaging reviews removed quickly, policy-based removal through our process delivers results that the legal system simply cannot match on timeline.
