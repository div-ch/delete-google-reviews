Policy Violations

Most harmful reviews violate platform policies

Platforms like Google have strict content guidelines. When a review crosses these lines, we build a case for removal based on documented violations.

Fake or Anonymous Reviews

Reviews from accounts with no history, fake names, or written by competitors violate authenticity policies.

Conflict of Interest

Reviews from employees, former employees, or parties in active legal disputes with your business are removable.

Off-Topic Content

Reviews that discuss irrelevant topics, personal vendettas, or unrelated businesses rather than a genuine customer experience.

Defamatory or False Claims

False statements of fact, fabricated incidents, or reviews intended to harm rather than inform can be flagged for removal.

Harassment or Hate Speech

Reviews containing threats, personal attacks, discriminatory language, or targeted harassment are clear policy violations.

Extortion or Coercion

Reviews left as leverage during disputes, or where the reviewer demanded payment or action in exchange for removal.

Common Questions

Everything you need to know

Our team reviews the content against each platform's official content policies. We look for specific violations including spam, fake content, off-topic reviews, conflict of interest, defamatory language, and inappropriate content. We will tell you upfront whether we believe a review is removable before you commit to anything.

Of all the reviews we formally pursue for removal, 94% are successfully taken down by the platform. The 6% that remain are typically reviews where the platform upheld the content despite our appeal. In those cases, you are not charged. The 94% figure is based on completed cases since 2012.

Most removals take between 2 and 8 weeks. Google typically responds to initial reports within 5 to 10 business days. If we escalate or appeal, the process can take longer. We keep you updated throughout and will never leave you in the dark on the status of your case.

Once a review is removed by the platform, it is permanently deleted from that page. The reviewer would have to post a new review, which would then be subject to the same content policies. In most cases, reviewers who had a review removed for violations do not repost.

Yes, completely legal. We use the official flagging and appeal channels that Google and other platforms provide for exactly this purpose. Reporting a review that violates content policies is a right every business owner has. We do not use any methods that would violate platform terms of service or risk your account standing.
