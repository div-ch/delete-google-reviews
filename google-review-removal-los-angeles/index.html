Legal Context

California Defamation Law and Online Reviews

California law provides significant protections for businesses that are the target of false and defamatory online reviews. Under California Civil Code Section 44, defamation is defined as a false statement of fact that injures a person's or business's reputation. Section 45 specifically addresses libel, which includes written statements published online such as Google reviews.

When Reviews Cross the Line from Opinion to Defamation

California courts distinguish between protected opinion and actionable defamation. A review that says "I did not enjoy my experience" is protected speech. A review that makes a specific false factual claim, such as falsely accusing a restaurant of health code violations, a doctor of malpractice, or an attorney of mishandling funds, crosses into potentially defamatory territory under California law. Our process focuses on identifying reviews that contain provably false statements of fact, which both violate Google's policies and potentially constitute defamation under California law.

Our Approach Prioritizes Speed Over Litigation

While California law provides the legal framework to challenge defamatory reviews in court, litigation is slow and expensive. Our approach focuses on Google's own content policies, which allows us to achieve removal in 3 to 21 business days rather than the months or years that litigation can require. For Los Angeles businesses that need results quickly, policy-based removal is almost always the faster and more cost-effective path. When a review involves particularly egregious defamation, we can coordinate with your legal team to pursue both policy-based removal and legal remedies simultaneously.
