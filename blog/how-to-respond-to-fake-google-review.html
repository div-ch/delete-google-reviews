How to Respond to a Fake Google Review While Waiting for Removal

Published April 12, 2026 • DeleteGoogleReviews.com

You have flagged a fake Google review. You have submitted your removal request. Now you are waiting. That period between flagging a review and getting it removed can last days, weeks, or sometimes longer. During that time, every potential customer who searches for your business will see that fake review. What you say in response to it matters enormously, both for how those potential customers perceive your business and for how Google's algorithm ranks your listing in local search results.

Why Responding to Fake Reviews Matters

Your first instinct might be to ignore a review you know is fake, especially if you have already flagged it and expect it to be removed. That instinct is understandable, but acting on it is a mistake for several reasons.

Customer Perception

Research consistently shows that potential customers pay close attention to how businesses respond to negative reviews. A BrightLocal survey found that 89% of consumers read business responses to reviews. When they see a thoughtful, professional response to a negative review, it actually increases their trust in the business. When they see no response at all, many assume the criticism is valid and that the business does not care enough to address it.

A well-crafted response to a fake review turns a negative into a positive. It shows potential customers that you are attentive, professional, and transparent. It also provides important context that the review itself does not contain, such as the fact that you have no record of the reviewer as a customer.

Google's Algorithm Considers Responses

Google's local search algorithm factors in several review-related signals when determining how to rank your business in the local pack and map results. These signals include your overall star rating, your review volume, and your response rate. Businesses that consistently respond to reviews tend to rank higher in local search results than businesses that leave reviews unanswered.

Google has stated publicly that responding to reviews shows engagement with your customers and is a positive signal for local search ranking. By responding to a fake review, you are not just managing the perception of that specific review. You are also sending a positive engagement signal to Google's ranking algorithm.

Legal Documentation

In some cases, your response to a fake review can serve as part of a documented record if you later pursue legal action against the reviewer. A calm, factual response that notes specific discrepancies, such as having no record of the reviewer as a customer, creates a timestamped record that supports your position.

The Golden Rules of Responding to Fake Reviews

Before getting into specific templates, here are the fundamental principles that should guide every response you write to a fake review. These rules apply regardless of the type of fake review you are dealing with.

  • Never accuse the reviewer of being fake publicly. Even if you are 100% certain the review is fraudulent, calling it out publicly makes you look defensive and aggressive. It can also provoke the reviewer into escalating the situation with additional reviews or social media attacks.
  • Never get emotional. Anger, sarcasm, and frustration are natural reactions to a fake review, but expressing them in your response will hurt your business more than the fake review itself. Potential customers reading your response are evaluating your professionalism and temperament.
  • Never reveal private information. Do not include any customer data, employee details, or confidential business information in your response, even if you believe doing so would prove the review is fake. Privacy violations in review responses can create legal liability and violate Google's policies.
  • Never threaten legal action publicly. Mentioning lawyers, lawsuits, or legal consequences in a public review response almost always backfires. It makes the business look threatening and litigious. If you intend to pursue legal action, do so privately through proper channels.
  • Keep it short. Aim for two to four sentences. Long responses draw more attention to the negative review and suggest that you are overly worried about it. A brief, confident response projects strength.
  • Include a call to action. End your response by inviting the reviewer to contact you directly. This shows good faith to anyone reading the response and, if the reviewer is truly fake, they will obviously never follow up.

Remember: You are not writing your response for the fake reviewer. You are writing it for every future customer who will read your reviews before deciding whether to contact your business. Write for that audience.

Response Templates by Scenario

Different types of fake reviews call for slightly different response strategies. Below are templates you can adapt for each common scenario. Customize these with your business name and specific details, but keep the tone and structure intact.

Competitor Attack

When you believe a review was posted by a competitor or someone associated with a competing business, your response should focus on the fact that you cannot verify the reviewer as a customer without directly accusing them of being a competitor.

Template: "Thank you for taking the time to leave feedback. We take all reviews seriously. After checking our records, we are unable to find any customer history associated with your name. We would like the opportunity to look into your experience further. Please contact us directly at [phone number] or [email] so we can investigate and address your concerns."

This response accomplishes several things. It signals to potential customers that the reviewer may not be a real customer. It projects professionalism and openness. And it invites direct contact that a fake reviewer will never pursue, which further undermines their credibility over time. For more details on handling competitor reviews, see our full guide on removing competitor-posted Google reviews.

Mistaken Identity

Some fake reviews are not malicious. The reviewer genuinely had a bad experience but left the review on the wrong business. This happens more often than you might expect, particularly for businesses with common names or businesses located near other businesses in the same industry.

Template: "We appreciate you sharing your feedback. We have searched our records thoroughly and cannot find any transaction or interaction matching the experience you described. It is possible that this review was intended for a different business. We would be happy to help sort this out. Please reach out to us at [phone number] or [email] and we will do our best to assist."

This response is gentle and gives the reviewer the benefit of the doubt. If they did post on the wrong business, this response may prompt them to remove the review themselves. If the review was malicious, this response still looks professional and raises a legitimate question about the reviewer's credibility without being confrontational.

Ex-Employee Review

Reviews from former employees are particularly tricky because they often contain enough insider knowledge to sound credible to outside readers. Your response needs to address the review without confirming the reviewer's identity or revealing any employment details.

Template: "Thank you for your feedback. Our review of records does not show you as a customer of our business. Google's review policies are designed for customer experiences, and we encourage all feedback to go through the appropriate channels. If you have concerns you would like to discuss, please contact us directly at [phone number]."

This response subtly signals that the reviewer may not be a customer without accusing them of being an ex-employee. It references Google's review policies, which prohibit conflict-of-interest reviews, without getting into specifics. For a complete guide on handling ex-employee reviews, read our article on what to do when an ex-employee leaves a bad Google review.

Troll or Spam Review

Some fake reviews come from accounts that post negative reviews across many businesses with no apparent connection to any of them. These are typically spam accounts or trolls who leave inflammatory reviews for entertainment or to sell review management services.

Template: "We appreciate all genuine customer feedback. We have no record of your visit to our business and are unable to identify the experience you described. If you are a customer of ours and we can help resolve a concern, please contact us at [phone number] or [email]. We are committed to providing an excellent experience for every customer."

This response is minimal and professional. It does not give the troll the reaction they may be looking for, while still providing context for legitimate customers reading the review.

Review with False Factual Claims

When a fake review makes specific false claims, such as alleging health code violations, criminal behavior, or professional misconduct, your response needs to be more direct without crossing the line into aggression or revealing confidential information.

Template: "We take feedback seriously, and we also take the accuracy of public claims about our business seriously. The specific claims in this review do not reflect our records or the experience our customers receive. We maintain [relevant certifications, inspection records, or other verifiable credentials]. We would welcome the opportunity to discuss this further. Please contact us at [phone number] or [email]."

This response addresses the false claims without repeating them, which is important because repeating false claims in your response can amplify them. It references verifiable facts that contradict the claims without going into detail that could be argumentative.

What NOT to Say in Your Response

The mistakes business owners make in review responses often cause more damage than the fake reviews themselves. Here are the most common pitfalls and why they are harmful.

Do Not Call the Review Fake

Phrases like "this review is clearly fake," "this person was never a customer," or "this is a competitor attack" sound defensive even when they are true. Potential customers reading the response have no way to verify your claim, and it comes across as a business owner trying to dismiss criticism rather than address it.

Do Not Get Personal

Never reference the reviewer's identity, location, employment status, or any personal details in your response. Even if you know exactly who left the review, sharing that information publicly can create legal liability and always makes the business look worse than the reviewer.

Do Not Write a Novel

Long, detailed responses attract more attention to the negative review and give the impression that you are rattled. The longer your response, the more weight potential customers give to the original review. Keep your response under four sentences in almost every case.

Do Not Respond Multiple Times

Respond once and leave it. If the reviewer replies to your response with additional negative comments, do not engage further. Ongoing back-and-forth threads in review responses always make the business look worse, regardless of who is right.

Do Not Copy and Paste the Same Response

If you have multiple fake reviews, do not use the identical response for each one. Google's algorithm can detect templated responses, and potential customers who notice identical replies across multiple reviews will question your authenticity. Customize each response even if the core message is similar.

How Review Responses Affect Local SEO Ranking

Your response strategy has a direct impact on your Google local search performance. Here is what the data shows about how review engagement affects ranking.

Response Rate Signal

Google tracks how frequently businesses respond to reviews. Businesses with higher response rates tend to perform better in local search rankings. According to multiple local SEO studies, businesses that respond to more than 50% of their reviews see measurably higher engagement on their Google Business Profile, including more direction requests, phone calls, and website visits.

Keyword Relevance in Responses

Your review responses are indexed by Google and can contribute to the keyword relevance of your Google Business Profile. When you mention your services, location, or specialties in review responses, you are adding relevant content that Google can use to understand what your business does and where it operates. This does not mean you should stuff keywords into responses. It means that naturally mentioning your business and services in context can provide a small but meaningful SEO benefit.

Engagement and Freshness

Google favors active, engaged business profiles. Responding to reviews adds fresh content to your profile and signals that the business is actively managed. Profiles that are regularly updated with responses rank higher than dormant profiles, all other factors being equal.

When to Respond vs. When to Stay Silent

While responding to reviews is generally advisable, there are rare situations where silence is the better choice.

Respond when: The review is visible on your profile and potential customers will see it. The review makes specific claims that could influence a potential customer's decision. You can craft a response that adds value for future readers.

Consider staying silent when: The review is so obviously spam that no reasonable person would take it seriously, such as a one-word review on a completely unrelated topic. Responding would draw attention to a review that most people would otherwise skip over. The reviewer has a pattern of responding aggressively to business responses, and engagement would create a visible argument thread.

In the vast majority of cases, a brief professional response is better than silence. The situations where silence is genuinely the best strategy are rare.

Timing Your Response

Speed matters. Respond to fake reviews within 24 to 48 hours of when they appear. Quick responses demonstrate that you are attentive and engaged. They also reduce the window during which potential customers see an unanswered negative review.

That said, do not respond while you are angry. If you discover a fake review and your first reaction is frustration, write a draft response, step away for an hour, and then revisit it. The response you write when you are calm will always be better than the one you write in the heat of the moment.

After the Review Is Removed

Once a fake review is removed through flagging, professional services, or legal channels, your response disappears along with it. There is no cleanup needed on your end. However, if you suspect the reviewer may post again, continue monitoring your reviews closely for the next several weeks.
