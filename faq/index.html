General
Professional review removal is a service where specialists analyze negative online reviews for policy violations and build documented cases for their removal. Rather than simply flagging a review and hoping the platform acts, professionals identify specific guideline violations, compile supporting evidence, and submit removal requests through the most effective channels. This approach produces significantly higher success rates than business owners attempting removal on their own.

Legal
In some cases, yes. If a review contains provably false statements of fact that damage your business, you may have grounds for a defamation lawsuit. However, litigation is expensive, time-consuming, and uncertain. Professional review removal through platform policy enforcement is typically faster, less expensive, and more reliable than legal action. We recommend exploring removal first. If legal action is warranted, we can work alongside your attorney to support both approaches simultaneously.
Review removal works through platform policy enforcement. We identify violations of a platform's terms of service and request removal based on those violations. Legal action involves filing a lawsuit for defamation or other claims and obtaining a court order. Removal is faster (days to weeks versus months to years), less expensive (custom pricing per review versus thousands in legal fees), and has a higher success rate for most cases. Legal action may be appropriate when the reviewer's identity is known and the damages are substantial enough to justify litigation costs.
